## 1. Executive Summary

This business plan outlines the formation and growth strategy for an IT Enabled Services (ITES) company headquartered in **Chennai, Tamil Nadu, India**. The company provides IT maintenance and support, application change management, FinOps consulting, cloud engineering consulting, and web development to **small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs)** across **India, Australia, and the Middle East**.

Founded by **four partners** — three investing partners located in India, Dubai, and Australia respectively, and one operations partner (Person-A) based in Chennai who manages day-to-day operations and leads FinOps consulting with a sweat equity stake — the company leverages India's cost advantage, a skilled talent pool in Chennai's thriving IT corridor, and the investing partners' regional presence to acquire and serve clients across three geographies.

The company provides **five complementary service lines**: IT Maintenance & Support, Application Change Management, FinOps Consulting, Cloud Engineering Consulting, and Web Development—enabling comprehensive digital transformation solutions for SMBs ranging from infrastructure optimization to custom application delivery.

The company will leverage **financial and operational support programs offered by the Tamil Nadu state government** (StartupTN, TANSIM, TIDCO, and related schemes) to minimise initial capital requirements, access subsidised infrastructure, and accelerate early-stage growth.

## 2. Company Overview

### 2.1 Legal Structure — Recommendation

#### Recommended: **Private Limited Company (Pvt Ltd)** under the Companies Act, 2013

Given the partnership structure (including international partners in Dubai and Australia), the following business structures were evaluated:

| Structure | Pros | Cons | Suitability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Private Limited Company (Pvt Ltd)** | FDI-compliant under automatic route; sweat equity provisions under Companies Act; best credibility with international SMB clients; clean equity structure; eligible for most TN government startup schemes; easy to issue ESOPs | Higher compliance burden (annual filings, audits); slightly higher setup cost | **Best Fit ✓** |
| **Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)** | Flexible profit sharing; lower compliance; tax efficient (no DDT); limited liability | FDI allowed only under government approval route (slower); harder to accommodate sweat equity cleanly; some TN schemes may prefer Pvt Ltd | Good alternative |
| **One Person Company (OPC)** | Simple setup | Only one member allowed; does not support partnerships | Not suitable ✗ |
| **Proprietorship** | Simplest setup | Unlimited liability; cannot accommodate partners; no credibility for international clients | Not suitable ✗ |
| **Partnership Firm** | Easy to form | Unlimited liability for partners; no FDI route; poor credibility for international clients | Not suitable ✗ |

#### Why Private Limited Company is the Best Fit:

1. **Foreign Direct Investment (FDI):** IT/ITES is under the 100% automatic route for FDI. Since two of the three investing partners are based in Dubai and Australia, a Pvt Ltd company allows them to invest and hold shares without requiring government approval.
2. **Sweat Equity for Person-A:** The Companies Act, 2013 (Section 54) explicitly provides for issuance of **sweat equity shares** — allowing Person-A to receive an equity stake in recognition of their operational contribution without capital investment. This is clean, legally sound, and well-understood by investors and auditors.
3. **Client Credibility:** SMB clients in Australia and the Middle East are more comfortable engaging with a registered Pvt Ltd company than an LLP or partnership firm. It signals permanence, governance, and accountability.
4. **Government Scheme Eligibility:** Most Tamil Nadu startup support schemes (StartupTN, TANSIM grants, TIDCO incubation) are designed for DPIIT-registered startups — Pvt Ltd companies are the default eligible entity type.
5. **Scalability:** Pvt Ltd structure supports future fundraising (angel/VC), ESOP pools for key employees, and potential conversion to a Public Limited Company if needed.

#### Equity Structure (Recommended):

| Partner | Role | Location | Capital Investment | Equity Stake |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Partner-B** | Investor + Business Development (India) | India | ₹X (equal share) | 25% |
| **Partner-C** | Investor + Business Development (Middle East) | Dubai, UAE | ₹X (equal share) | 25% |
| **Partner-D** | Investor + Business Development (Australia) | Australia | ₹X (equal share) | 25% |
| **Person-A** | Operations Partner (Sweat Equity) | Chennai, India | Nil | 25% (Sweat Equity Shares) |

> **Note:** The exact equity split can be adjusted. An alternative model is 30% each for the three investors and 10% sweat equity for Person-A, vesting over 3–4 years. The recommended split above (equal 25%) is proposed to incentivise Person-A who bears the full operational burden.
>
> **Accelerated Cliff Option:** Person-A may voluntarily purchase shares at fair market valuation during the 1-year cliff period (up to the 25% stake limit). Each purchase proportionally reduces the remaining cliff duration — enabling Person-A to accelerate equity ownership by investing personal capital before the cliff expires. See Section 10.6 for detailed terms.

#### Key Legal Steps:

1. Register Pvt Ltd company with MCA (Ministry of Corporate Affairs) — obtain CIN
2. Register for DPIIT Startup Recognition (eligible for tax benefits under Section 80-IAC, angel tax exemption)
3. Obtain GST registration and Professional Tax registration in Tamil Nadu
4. Open company bank account and receive FDI from Partner-C (Dubai) and Partner-D (Australia) via inward remittance through AD (Authorised Dealer) bank
5. File FC-GPR (Foreign Currency – Gross Provisional Return) with RBI within 30 days of share allotment to foreign partners
6. Issue Sweat Equity shares to Person-A following Section 54 of the Companies Act (requires special resolution and valuation report)

## 4. Market Analysis

### 4.1 Industry Overview

The global IT services market is projected to exceed **USD 1.5 trillion by 2028** (Gartner). Within this, the managed IT services and cloud consulting segments are among the fastest growing, driven by:
- SMBs increasingly outsourcing IT operations to focus on core business
- Cloud adoption accelerating across all geographies and company sizes
- Rising complexity of multi-cloud environments creating demand for specialised consulting
- FinOps emerging as a critical discipline as cloud spend grows uncontrolled

### 4.2 Target Markets

#### India
- **Market Size:** India's domestic IT services market is estimated at **USD 50+ billion** (NASSCOM 2025), with SMBs representing a large and underserved segment.
- **Opportunity:** Most Indian SMBs (₹5 Cr – ₹500 Cr revenue) lack a dedicated IT team. They rely on ad-hoc local vendors for maintenance and have no structured cloud strategy. There is significant demand for reliable, affordable IT support with SLA-backed accountability.
- **Key Cities:** Chennai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Mumbai, Pune, NCR.
- **Industries:** Manufacturing, retail/e-commerce, logistics, healthcare, financial services, education.

#### Australia
- **Market Size:** Australia's IT services market is valued at **AUD 45+ billion** (IBISWorld 2025).
- **Opportunity:** Australian SMBs face a **severe IT talent shortage** and **high local labour costs** (AUD 120–180/hour for IT professionals). Offshore/nearshore IT support from India offers 60–70% cost savings. Time zone overlap between Chennai (IST) and Australian Eastern Time (AEST) is manageable (~4.5–5.5 hours difference), allowing overlapping working hours.
- **Key Cities:** Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth.
- **Industries:** Mining/resources, financial services, retail, healthcare, professional services, construction.

#### Middle East (UAE / GCC)
- **Market Size:** The GCC IT services market is estimated at **USD 20+ billion** (IDC 2025), with UAE and Saudi Arabia being the largest markets.
- **Opportunity:** Rapid digital transformation (UAE Digital Government Strategy, Saudi Vision 2030) is driving cloud adoption. SMBs in the region are moving from on-premise to cloud but lack operational expertise. Indian IT companies are well-established and trusted in the region.
- **Key Cities:** Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Riyadh, Doha.
- **Industries:** Trading, logistics, real estate, financial services, hospitality, oil & gas services, retail.

### 4.3 Competitive Landscape

| Competitor Type | Examples | Our Advantage |
|---|---|---|
| **Large IT Services Firms** | TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Cognizant | They focus on large enterprises. SMBs are underserved and often find their minimum engagement sizes too high. We are **right-sized for SMBs**. |
| **Local MSPs (Managed Service Providers)** | Small local IT shops in each market | They lack scale, specialised cloud/FinOps capabilities, and SLA-backed service. We offer **structured, professional service** at competitive rates. |
| **Freelancers / Independent Contractors** | Upwork, Freelancer | No continuity, no SLAs, key-person dependency. We provide **team-based delivery** with backups and knowledge management. |
| **Cloud-native Consultancies** | Rackspace, Accenture Cloud First | Expensive, enterprise-focused. We offer **affordable cloud consulting** specifically designed for SMB budgets and complexity levels. |

### 4.4 SWOT Analysis

| | **Helpful** | **Harmful** |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal** | **Strengths:** Chennai talent pool; India cost advantage; three-geography partner presence; diverse service portfolio (IT support + cloud + FinOps); lean startup structure | **Weaknesses:** No brand recognition; no track record; small initial team; dependency on founding partners for business development; Person-A's bandwidth as sole operations lead |
| **External** | **Opportunities:** SMB cloud adoption accelerating; IT talent shortage in Australia/Middle East; TN government startup support; remote work normalisation post-COVID making offshore delivery widely accepted | **Threats:** Intense competition from established players; currency fluctuation risk (AUD, AED); client data security/compliance concerns; economic slowdown affecting SMB IT budgets; visa/travel restrictions |

## 5. Services & Offerings

### 5.1 IT Maintenance & Support

**Description:** Ongoing IT infrastructure and application support services delivered as a managed service with defined SLAs.

| Sub-Service | Details |
|---|---|
| **Infrastructure Monitoring & Management** | 24/7 monitoring of servers, networks, databases, and cloud resources; proactive issue detection and resolution |
| **Helpdesk & End-User Support** | L1/L2 support for employee IT issues — hardware, software, connectivity, access management |
| **System Administration** | OS patching, security updates, backup management, user provisioning, Active Directory/IAM management |
| **Database Administration** | Performance tuning, backup/restore, monitoring, query optimisation, version upgrades |
| **Security Operations** | Vulnerability scanning, firewall management, endpoint protection, security incident response |
| **Periodic Health Checks** | Quarterly infrastructure reviews, capacity planning, technology refresh recommendations |

**Delivery Model:** Offshore (Chennai) with remote access to client environments. Onsite support available for India-based and Middle East clients as needed.

**Pricing Model:** Monthly retainer based on scope (number of servers, users, applications).

### 5.2 Application Change Management

**Description:** Ongoing application maintenance, enhancements, and change management for business applications.

| Sub-Service | Details |
|---|---|
| **Bug Fixes & Incident Resolution** | Triage, root cause analysis, and resolution of application defects with SLA-based response and resolution times |
| **Minor Enhancements** | Small feature additions, UI/UX improvements, report modifications, workflow changes |
| **Major Change Requests** | Scoped and estimated as mini-projects — new modules, integrations, data migrations |
| **Release Management** | Structured release processes — dev, test, UAT, production deployment with rollback plans |
| **Application Testing** | Functional testing, regression testing, performance testing for changes |
| **Documentation** | Technical documentation, user guides, runbooks, knowledge base maintenance |

**Technologies:** .NET, Java, Python, PHP, Node.js, React, Angular, SQL Server, PostgreSQL, MySQL, Oracle, SAP (basis support), Salesforce (admin support), custom/legacy applications.

**Delivery Model:** Offshore (Chennai) with client collaboration via Teams/Slack/Jira. Onsite visits for discovery and UAT as needed.

**Pricing Model:** Time & Material (T&M) for ad-hoc changes; monthly retainer with a pool of hours for ongoing maintenance.

### 5.3 FinOps Consulting

**Description:** Help SMBs understand, optimise, and govern their cloud spending across AWS, Azure, and GCP.

| Sub-Service | Details |
|---|---|
| **Cloud Cost Assessment** | Baseline current cloud spend; identify waste, idle resources, over-provisioned instances, and unattached storage/IPs |
| **Optimisation Roadmap** | Prioritised recommendations with estimated savings — rightsizing, reserved instances, savings plans, spot usage, scheduling |
| **Cost Allocation & Tagging** | Design and implement tagging taxonomy for cost allocation by project, department, environment, and application |
| **Budget & Governance Setup** | Configure cloud-native budgeting tools, alerts, and approval workflows; establish spending guardrails |
| **Ongoing FinOps Advisory** | Monthly reviews of cloud spend trends, anomaly analysis, commitment utilisation, and optimisation tracking |
| **FinOps Training** | Workshops for client teams on cloud cost management principles and tools |

**Target Savings:** 20–40% reduction in cloud spend for clients with no existing FinOps practice.

**Delivery Model:** Primarily remote with initial on-site/virtual workshops for discovery. Monthly advisory delivered via video calls and reports.

**Pricing Model:** Fixed-fee for assessments and roadmaps; monthly retainer for ongoing advisory.

### 5.4 Cloud Engineering Consulting

**Description:** Help SMBs design, migrate to, and optimise cloud infrastructure on AWS, Azure, and GCP.

| Sub-Service | Details |
|---|---|
| **Cloud Readiness Assessment** | Evaluate existing infrastructure and applications for cloud migration; identify dependencies, risks, and migration strategies (rehost, refactor, rearchitect) |
| **Cloud Architecture Design** | Design scalable, secure, and cost-effective cloud architectures — networking, compute, storage, security, identity, monitoring |
| **Cloud Migration** | Plan and execute migrations — server migrations, database migrations, application re-platforming; minimal downtime strategies |
| **Infrastructure as Code (IaC)** | Implement Terraform, CloudFormation, Bicep, or Pulumi for automated, repeatable infrastructure provisioning |
| **DevOps & CI/CD** | Set up CI/CD pipelines (GitHub Actions, Azure DevOps, Jenkins, GitLab CI); containerisation (Docker, Kubernetes); monitoring (Prometheus, Grafana, CloudWatch) |
| **Cloud Security & Compliance** | Security hardening, IAM best practices, encryption, compliance alignment (SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR basics for SMBs) |
| **Managed Cloud Operations** | Ongoing cloud operations — monitoring, patching, scaling, incident response, cost management |

**Cloud Platforms:** AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud Platform (GCP). Multi-cloud and hybrid-cloud scenarios supported.

**Delivery Model:** Blended (onsite discovery + offshore execution). Heavy offshore leverage for implementation work.

**Pricing Model:** Fixed-fee for assessments and design; T&M for migration and implementation; monthly retainer for managed operations.

### 5.5 Web Development

**Description:** Custom web development using modern frameworks, with tiered service offerings designed for SMBs. Solutions range from template-based starter applications to fully customized, integrated enterprise-grade web platforms.

| Offering | Details |
|---|---|
| **Starter Tier** | Template-based, minimal customization, quick deployment, cost-effective entry point for SMBs. Pre-built templates for common use cases (e-commerce stores, CRM dashboards, service booking platforms). 2-4 week delivery. Fixed-scope projects. |
| **Standard Tier** | Moderate customization, integration with existing systems, bespoke UI/UX design, API connectivity. Suitable for SMBs with specific business requirements. 4-8 week delivery. Defined scope with limited flexibility. |
| **Pro Tier** | Fully custom development, advanced integrations (payment gateways, third-party APIs, custom backends), advanced features (analytics, reporting, automation). Enterprise-grade security and scalability. 8-16 week or longer delivery. Full project flexibility. |

**Key Features:**
- **Technology Stack:** React, Angular, Vue.js (frontend); Node.js, Python, PHP, .NET (backend); PostgreSQL, MySQL, MongoDB (databases)
- **Full-Stack Development:** Frontend UI/UX + backend API development
- **Integration Capabilities:** Payment processing (Stripe, PayPal), CRM integration, ERP connectivity, third-party APIs
- **Responsive Design:** Mobile-first, cross-browser compatible web applications
- **Post-Launch Support:** Maintenance packages, bug fixes, feature enhancements, performance optimization
- **Scalability:** Cloud-ready architecture, containerized deployment (Docker), CI/CD integration

**Delivery Model:** Blended onshore-offshore with offshore development from Chennai. Discovery and design workshops conducted onsite with client stakeholders or virtually. Regular sprint demos and feedback cycles (weekly or bi-weekly). Post-launch support available via offshore team.

**Pricing Model:**
- **Starter Tier:** Fixed-fee pricing (₹3-8 Lakhs depending on template complexity and customization)
- **Standard Tier:** Fixed-fee or T&M hybrid (₹8-20 Lakhs for defined scope projects)
- **Pro Tier:** T&M or project-based fixed-fee (₹20-100+ Lakhs depending on scope and complexity)
- **Post-Launch Support:** Monthly retainer for maintenance and enhancements (₹50,000 - ₹3,00,000/month based on support level)

**Project Cycle:**
1. **Discovery & Requirements:** Understand business needs, user personas, competitive landscape (1-2 weeks)
2. **Design & Prototyping:** Wireframes, UI/UX mockups, architecture design (1-2 weeks)
3. **Development:** Agile sprints (2-week cycles), weekly demos, iterative feedback (4-12 weeks depending on tier)
4. **Testing & QA:** Comprehensive functional, performance, security testing (1-2 weeks)
5. **Deployment & Launch:** Production deployment, user training, knowledge transfer (1 week)
6. **Post-Launch Support:** Bug fixes, enhancements, monitoring, performance tuning (ongoing)

**Target Use Cases:**
- E-commerce platforms (webshops, multi-vendor marketplaces)
- Business management applications (invoicing, project management, HR portals)
- Customer portals (service request tracking, self-service support)
- Dashboard and analytics platforms
- SaaS applications and white-label solutions
- Mobile-responsive web applications

**Team Composition (Typical Engagement):**
- 1 Business Analyst / Product Owner
- 1-2 Frontend Developers (React/Angular/Vue.js)
- 1-2 Backend Developers (Node.js/Python/.NET)
- 1 QA Engineer
- 1 DevOps Engineer (for deployment and CI/CD)
- 1 UI/UX Designer (for Standard and Pro tiers)

| **Dividends** | Distributed from profits after maintaining 6-month operating reserve. Pro-rata based on equity holding. |
| **Sweat Equity Vesting (Person-A)** | 25% equity vesting over 4 years with 1-year cliff. If Person-A leaves before cliff, equity reverts. **Accelerated Cliff Provision:** Person-A may voluntarily purchase shares (at the board-approved fair market valuation) at any time during the cliff period, up to the 25% stake limit. Each such purchase proportionally reduces the remaining cliff period. For example, if Person-A buys 50% of the cliff-period stake (i.e., 3.125% of total equity, being half of the 6.25% that would vest at the cliff) within the first 6 months, the cliff is considered 50% satisfied — reducing the remaining cliff from 12 months to 6 months. Once the cliff is fully satisfied (through time served, partial purchases, or a combination), monthly vesting of the remaining unvested shares begins on the standard 4-year schedule. Shares purchased during the cliff period are fully owned immediately and are not subject to vesting or clawback. |

> **Accelerated Cliff — Key Terms:**
> - **Valuation:** Share price for voluntary purchases during the cliff is determined by the Board based on a CA-certified valuation report (DCF or Net Asset method), refreshed at least annually.
> - **Pro-rata reduction formula:** Remaining cliff months = 12 × (1 − proportion of cliff-period stake purchased). E.g., buying 25% of the cliff stake → cliff reduces to 9 months; buying 100% → cliff is eliminated entirely.
> - **Cap:** Total shares held by Person-A (purchased + vested) shall not exceed the agreed 25% stake at any point.
> - **Board approval:** Each purchase requires Board resolution and compliance with Section 54/62 of the Companies Act, 2013.
> - **SHA clause:** This provision must be documented in the Shareholders' Agreement with clear mechanics, valuation methodology, and anti-dilution protections.
